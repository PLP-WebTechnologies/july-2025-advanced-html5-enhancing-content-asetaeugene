Wooden based chair
  • Material & Texture: Made from natural hardwoods like oak, teak, or mahogany, often showcasing beautiful grain patterns that vary with each piece.
  • Aesthetic Appeal: Offers a rustic, classic, or even artistic vibe depending on the craftsmanship. Can be ornately carved or sleek and minimalistic.
  • Durability: Strong and resilient, especially with well-seasoned wood. Some types may be susceptible to humidity or termite damage over time.
  • Comfort: The wood's natural temperature feel is usually pleasant, and its slight flex can add to seating comfort‚Äîoften paired with cushions for extra coziness.
  • Style Versatility: Blends beautifully into traditional, Scandinavian, or vintage interiors.

Metal based chair
  • Material & Finish: Constructed from aluminum, steel, or iron, often finished with powder coating, chrome, or paint for visual flair and rust resistance.
  • Design Language: Emphasizes clean lines and modern aesthetics, sometimes industrial or minimalist. Can include bold geometric patterns or perforated detailing.
  • Sturdiness: Exceptionally strong and often heavier depending on the metal. More resilient to the elements, making them ideal for both indoor and outdoor use.
  • Comfort: Typically firmer than wood‚Äîoften designed with ergonomic curves or paired with mesh, padding, or fabric upholstery for improved comfort.
  • Maintenance: Easy to clean and less vulnerable to pests or rot, though may need protection against corrosion in humid environments.
